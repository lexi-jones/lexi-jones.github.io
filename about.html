I am currently a Postdoctoral Associate in the Earth, Atmospheric, and Planetary Sciences (EAPS) Department at MIT, working in the Darwin Project Group led by Dr. Mick Follows. I have a Biological Oceanography Ph.D. from the MIT-WHOI Joint Program (2024) and a Mathematics B.A. with Minors in Physics and Business from Temple University (2018).

Research Interests

My research focus is on the role of mesoscale circulations in shaping phytoplankton communities from a Lagrangian perspective. The big picture goal is to ultimately understand how the ubiquitous mesoscale interactions in the ocean aggregate to support global biogeochemical cycles and the ocean food web. Though this work, I bridge physical and biological oceanography and use a combination of satellite remote sensing observations, in situ observations (e.g. amplicon sequencing, flow cytometry, autonomous vehicles), and numerical simulations. For more details and links to my publications, see my Research page.

Background

My research career began in Rob Kulthinal's Evolutionary Genetics and Biological Informatics lab at Temple University.
